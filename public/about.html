About Chaudhary Devi Lal University

Discover our journey, mission, and commitment to excellence

Introduction

Chaudhary Devi Lal University (CDLU), Sirsa, is a premier state university established by the Government of Haryana under Act No. 9 of 2003. The university is named after Jan Nayak Chaudhary Devi Lal, the former Deputy Prime Minister of India, who was a visionary leader and a champion of social justice.

Spread across a sprawling 213-acre campus on Barnala Road, Sirsa, the university is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 12(B) and is a member of the Association of Indian Universities (AIU).

CDLU is connected to the National Knowledge Network (NKN) via a 1 GBPS optical fiber link, providing high-speed internet access to students and faculty. The campus is Wi-Fi enabled with modern infrastructure including four teaching blocks, Vivekananda Library, separate hostels for boys and girls, a health centre, sports facilities, and a community radio station.

Vision & Mission

Guiding principles that shape our academic journey

Our Vision

To be a leading centre of higher education and research, fostering intellectual growth, innovation, and social responsibility among students.

Our Mission

To provide quality education that is knowledge-based, value-oriented, and skill-focused, preparing students for global challenges.

Our Values

Academic excellence, inclusivity, transparency, innovation, and commitment to social justice and national development.
