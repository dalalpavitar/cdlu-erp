History & Timeline

The journey of CDLU from inception to the present

Our Journey

Chaudhary Devi Lal University (CDLU), Sirsa, was established by the Government of Haryana under Act No. 9 of 2003. The university is named in honour of Jan Nayak Chaudhary Devi Lal, a former Deputy Prime Minister of India, who dedicated his life to the service of farmers, labourers, and marginalized sections of society.

Since its inception, the university has grown from strength to strength, expanding its academic offerings, infrastructure, and research capabilities. Today, CDLU stands as a premier institution of higher learning in the state of Haryana.

Key Milestones

2003

Establishment of CDLU

Chaudhary Devi Lal University was established by the Government of Haryana under Act 9 of 2003 with a vision to provide quality higher education to the region.

2004

Commencement of Academic Programmes

The university started its first academic programmes with a focus on arts, sciences, and commerce disciplines.

2008

UGC Recognition under 12(B)

CDLU was recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 12(B), enabling it to receive central assistance for development.

2012

Expansion of Campus Infrastructure

New teaching blocks, Vivekananda Library, and separate hostels for boys and girls were constructed, significantly expanding campus capacity.

2016

Establishment of New Departments

Several new teaching departments were established, including Computer Science, Management Studies, Law, and Education.

2020

Digital Transformation

The university implemented a comprehensive ERP system and shifted to online teaching-learning platforms in response to the global pandemic.

2023

20 Years of Excellence

CDLU celebrated two decades of academic excellence with over 42,000 students enrolled across 24 departments and 62+ affiliated colleges.

2026

Continued Growth & Innovation

The university continues to expand its academic programmes, research output, and infrastructure, embracing NEP 2020 and digital education.
